The Story behind Iraqi Women Rights Organization

Tamara Amir Noori, A young woman born in Kuwait 1987, moved with her parents to Denmark In 2001. She is raised in an active sport family; her father was the Iraqi boxing champion back in 1967, Karate and Aikido trainer . Her brother became the Danish Amateur Boxing Champion 2015. While she was working for different NGO’s in Copenhagen, she was exposed to a different world of injustice towards women like herself. Abandoned and neglected women who did not have any resource of help
In 2011 she created a Facebook page, under the name (Iraqi Women Institution), dedicated to sharing different ideas and thoughts that could affect ociety positively. In 2012 the name was changed to (Iraqi Women Rights) that positioned itself solely to talking about women rights in Iraq. In 2014, Tamara started posting on Facebook on a daily basis to bring attention to the crisis that a lot of women are suffering injustice at the hands of the government, spouses or even family members. Her colleague Thuraya Rifat joined her as an admin on the page shortly after. Both were very active in posting different social problems to raise awareness about human and women’s rights in the community. They were also offering advice to women living under violence and bad conditions.

In 2015 the page of Iraqi women rights became very successful and well-known, positive reviews started to increase, people started to believe in us and participate with their ideas to solve problems (Brain Storming) in our mission to fight the whole ideology of the (one gender society). IWRO was officially registered in Denmark in 2015, CVR: 37167681.
In 2016 IWRO created Instagram page under name Iraqi_Women_Rights, which become very large days now. and in the same year, some prominent Iraqi lawyers joined us as our attorneys and legal advisors from Baghdad working with us (voluntarily) on the most difficult cases.
IWR became a very strong and diverse team, consisting of educated and open-minded activists that posts and works for the oppressed Iraqi women from across the globe.
